About Our Temple

Our Sacred Temple

Shri Anjaneyaswamy Seva Sami is a sacred temple dedicated to Shri Mukhyaprana Devaru, consecrated by Shri Vyasaraja. This holy place stands as a symbol of devotion, culture, and tradition. The temple continues to inspire devotees through its rich heritage, spiritual practices, and divine atmosphere.

Our mission is to preserve this ancient place of worship and serve the spiritual needs of the community while maintaining the highest standards of Hindu religious practices.

Our Sacred History

1500-1501 AD

Prahlada Raja, the supreme devotee who won the grace of Lord Narasimha, incarnated in the Kali Yuga as Shri Vyasaraja Swamiji for the upliftment of noble souls. Across the length and breadth of India, he consecrated thousands of idols of Shri Mukhyaprana Devaru (who is worshipped as Anjaneya, Bhimasena, and Madhvacharya in unity).

Divine Consecration

In the year Shaka 1422 (A.D. 1500–1501), during the Raudri Samvatsara, Shri Vyasaraja attained the unique glory of consecrating 732 idols of Shri Mukhyaprana in a single year, strictly following the rituals laid down in the Tantrasara Sangraha of Jagadguru Shri Madhvacharya.

Spiritual Legacy

Furthermore, Shri Vyasaraja composed numerous stotras (hymns) and songs in praise of Shri Mukhyaprana, who resides within all living beings, enabling vital functions such as breathing and sustaining life itself.
